About The Club

About The Club

Tech Spark at IILM University, Greater Noida, is a dynamic and innovative student-led club dedicated to fostering a culture of technology, creativity, and collaboration. The club serves as a hub for tech enthusiasts, bringing together like-minded individuals who are passionate about exploring the latest advancements in technology and applying them to real-world challenges. The club organizes a diverse range of activities, including tech events, hands-on workshops, hackathons, and inter-college competitions. These events are designed to provide students with practical exposure to various aspects of technology, ranging from coding and software development to emerging trends like artificial intelligence, machine learning, and blockchain.Tech Spark is more than just a club it's a vibrant ecosystem where students can learn, grow, and thrive together, while staying at the forefront of technological innovation. .

F.A.Q

  • What is Tech Spark?

    Tech Spark is a student-led club at IILM University, Greater Noida, aimed at fostering a culture of technology, creativity, and collaboration. It organizes tech events, workshops, hackathons, and competitions, providing students with practical exposure to technology.

  • What is the main focus of TechVision 2.0?

    TechVision 2.0 is a tech festival celebrating technology, creativity, and collaboration, featuring events such as research paper presentations, ideation challenges, and gaming competitions.

  • What is the INNOQUEST event?

    INNOQUEST is a tech-based model solution presentation event where teams create and showcase innovative models. It focuses on themes like healthcare, cybersecurity, sustainability, AI, and IoT, with prizes awarded to the top teams.

  • What is the structure of the Algo Ninja Challenge?

    The Algo Ninja Challenge is a coding competition involving two rounds. Round 1 features multiple-choice coding questions, and Round 2 presents advanced algorithm-based problems. The top teams are awarded cash prizes.

  • What does the Workshop on Blockchain cover?

    The workshop introduces students to blockchain fundamentals, including a hands-on session and case studies from industries like finance, healthcare, and supply chain. The session includes practical coding experience in Python.

  • What is the Research Paper Symposium?

    The Research Paper Symposium allows participants to present their research in various tech fields. Teams present their papers in a 10-minute session, followed by a Q&A. Presentations are judged on innovation, methodology, and results.

  • What are the rules for the Call of Duty Gaming Tournament?

    The Call of Duty tournament is a team-based event where participants compete in multiplayer modes like Team Deathmatch and Search & Destroy. The event promotes teamwork and is structured in knockout rounds.

  • What is the participation structure for the INNOQUEST event?

    Teams of up to four members can participate. The event includes registration and screening, followed by model presentations, with evaluation criteria based on innovation, feasibility, and alignment with the event themes.

  • What is the registration process for TechVision events?
